Course Description

This course introduces students to decision-making and model building in the management of operations which create products and/or services. The principles of operations management apply throughout the world to all productive enterprises. Efficient production methods require the effective application of the concepts, tools, and techniques that are covered in this course. Hands-on learning is an important feature of the course. Project case analyses will require students to collect, organize and analyze data to provide recommendations for how to improve the performance of a specified operating or supply chain system.

Prerequisites

AIMS or BCOR 2710 or ACCT 3140, ECON 2300 or MATH 104, MATH 112 or 131, all minimum grade of C

Learning Outcomes

CBA Core Learning Outcomes

This course contributes to student achievement of the following CBA core learning outcomes:

  • Students will be able to analyze business processes to better serve stakeholders.
  • Students will be able to frame a business problem and collect, organize and analyze qualitative and quantitative data to report and communicate insights for decision-making.
  • Students will be able to apply information technology to solve business problems
  • Students will demonstrate critical thinking skills by defining, modeling, analyzing and evaluating complex business problems

Course Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course students are expected to successfully meet the following operations & supply chain management analytics course learning outcomes:

  • Understand the common principles of production and operations management.
  • Apply strategies dealing with various problems encountered in the production of goods and services.
  • Perform data processing and visualization.
  • Use analytical techniques and a statistical package to support business decisions.
  • Report and communicate insights and provide recommendations for an operations-related problem.

Tips for Succeeding in Class

5 Keys to Success in This Course

Stay Present & Engaged

Show up — in person, on Teams, and in conversations. The more visible you are, the more I can coach you toward success. Disappearing is the fastest way to fall behind.

Bring Solutions, Not Just Problems

Challenges will come up: a tricky assignment, a group conflict, a late submission. When you reach out, also suggest a possible solution. This shows initiative and makes it easier for us to problem-solve together.

Build a Professional Relationship

Treat this class like a career opportunity. Ask questions, connect with me on LinkedIn, and seek feedback. Faculty often recommend students for jobs, but only if they know and trust you.

Practice Professionalism Daily

How you write emails and Teams messages, present your work, and collaborate with peers reflects directly on your future career. Use this class as a low-risk environment to practice the habits employers value.

Find Your Voice

Speaking up in class isn't the only way to contribute. Use Teams chat, post questions, or share resources. What matters is showing that you are actively learning and thinking — not staying silent.

Policies

Artificial Intelligence (AI) Policy

AI is a core part of this course and your future career

I describe myself as an AI optimist: I believe these tools will shape the future of work and create new opportunities, but I also know they can be misused if relied on without thought. In this class, AI is treated the same way employers are starting to treat it, as a tool, not a substitute for your judgment.


We will be using AI throughout the course. My goal is to help you learn how to use it effectively and responsibly, and to make your ability to use AI a skill differentiator that sets you apart in internships and job interviews.

✅ You may use AI to:

  • Brainstorm ideas or outlines for assignments
  • Reformat, polish, or reword text you've already written
  • Automate routine tasks (for example, cleaning or formatting data in Excel)
  • Explore alternative approaches to a problem (but not copy-paste answers)

❌ You may not use AI to:

  • Generate complete assignments, projects, or exam answers and submit them as your own
  • Avoid learning the underlying skills we're practicing (for example, running a regression, building a forecast, designing a visualization)
  • Hide your use of AI. Transparency matters

Expectations:

  • If you use AI in your work, disclose it (for example, "I used ChatGPT to help me brainstorm project ideas")
  • I grade your understanding and ability to explain your process, not just the final answer. If you can't explain your solution without the tool, you haven't met the learning goal
  • Think of AI as a junior analyst — useful, but still needs your oversight

Bottom Line

AI can give you leverage, but your critical thinking, decision-making, and ability to explain your work are what will make you stand out. Use this course to practice turning AI from a shortcut into a competitive advantage.
