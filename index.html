Course Information

Course Description

This course introduces students to decision-making and model building in the management of operations which create products and/or services. The principles of operations management apply throughout the world to all productive enterprises. Efficient production methods require the effective application of the concepts, tools, and techniques that are covered in this course. Hands-on learning is an important feature of the course. Project case analyses will require students to collect, organize and analyze data to provide recommendations for how to improve the performance of a specified operating or supply chain system.

Prerequisites

AIMS or BCOR 2710 or ACCT 3140, ECON 2300 or MATH 104, MATH 112 or 131, all minimum grade of C

Policies

ü§ñ Artificial Intelligence (AI) Policy

AI is a core part of this course and your future career

I describe myself as an AI optimist: I believe these tools will shape the future of work and create new opportunities, but I also know they can be misused if relied on without thought. In this class, AI is treated the same way employers are starting to treat it, as a tool, not a substitute for your judgment.

We will be using AI throughout the course. My goal is to help you learn how to use it effectively and responsibly, and to make your ability to use AI a skill differentiator that sets you apart in internships and job interviews.

‚úÖ You may use AI to:

  • Brainstorm ideas or outlines for assignments
  • Reformat, polish, or reword text you've already written
  • Automate routine tasks (for example, cleaning or formatting data in Excel)
  • Explore alternative approaches to a problem (but not copy-paste answers)

‚ùå You may not use AI to:

  • Generate complete assignments, projects, or exam answers and submit them as your own
  • Avoid learning the underlying skills we're practicing (for example, running a regression, building a forecast, designing a visualization)
  • Hide your use of AI. Transparency matters

üìã Expectations:

  • If you use AI in your work, disclose it (for example, "I used ChatGPT to help me brainstorm project ideas")
  • I grade your understanding and ability to explain your process, not just the final answer. If you can't explain your solution without the tool, you haven't met the learning goal
  • Think of AI as a junior analyst ‚Äî useful, but still needs your oversight

üéØ Bottom Line

AI can give you leverage, but your critical thinking, decision-making, and ability to explain your work are what will make you stand out. Use this course to practice turning AI from a shortcut into a competitive advantage.
